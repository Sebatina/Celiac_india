What is Celiac disease?

Celiac disease is an autoimmune disease thatâ€™s activated when a person consumes gluten, which damages the villi which is present in the small intestine. Gluten is a protein found in wheat, rye, and barley. When people with celiac disease eat foods containing gluten, their immune system responds by damaging the lining of the small intestine. This results in difficulty absorbing nutrients from food, leading to various symptoms.

Symptoms

Ranges from sever malabsorption to minimally symptomatic presentations, they are:

  • Diarrhea
  • Fatigue
  • Weight Loss
  • Bloating
  • Abdominal Pain                
  • Nausea
  • Constipation
  • Impaired Growth
  • Anemia
  • Osteoporosis

Risk Factors

Factors that might pose a risk of developing celiac disease:

  • A family member with celiac disease
  • HLA-DQ2 and HLA-D8 genes
  • Certain Genetic Disorder
  • Other Autoimmune diseases

Diagnosis

  • Serology testing
  • Tissue transglutaminase (TG2)

Treatment

The primary treatment for celiac disease is gluten-free food. This means avoiding all sources of gluten, which is found in wheat, rye, and barley. Currently there is no treatment available for celiac disease. Researchers are working hard to find a cure for this disease.

Celiac disease in India

Celiac disease is a common disorder in India, with a prevalence of about 8.5%. In northern India, 1% of the population is affected by celiac disease and about half of that in other parts of the country. However, this figure may be underestimated, as many cases of celiac disease go undiagnosed or misdiagnosed. This is because the symptoms of celiac disease can be similar to other digestive disorders, such as irritable bowel syndrome, and the diagnosis of celiac disease requires specific blood tests and a biopsy of the small intestine.

One of the main challenges in the management of celiac disease in India is the limited availability of gluten-free products. The cereal consumption patterns are very different between north and south India, although there has been a recent change in these patterns particularly in urban areas. In south India, rice is the primary cereal consumed in the diet. In northern India, wheat is a staple food and is used in many traditional dishes, making it difficult for individuals with celiac disease to avoid gluten. This can lead to poor compliance with the gluten-free diet, which is the only treatment for celiac disease. In addition, gluten-free products are often expensive and not widely available, making it difficult for individuals with celiac disease to follow the diet.

Marriage between first or second-degree relatives, also known as consanguineous marriage, is a common practice in many parts of India. This type of marriage occurs when two individuals are related by blood, either through a common ancestor or through a shared sibling. Consanguineous marriage is believed to be more common in India due to cultural, social, and economic factors. This type of marriage can increase the risk of genetic disorders and inherited medical conditions in children. Statistics show that 2.2 million children under the age group of 5 have undiagnosed celiac disease.

To improve the management of celiac disease in India, it is important to increase the awareness and understanding of the disorder among the general population and the healthcare sector. In addition, it is important to increase the availability and affordability of gluten-free products in India.
