One-Stop Database

Celiac disease (CeD) is an autoimmune disease induced by gluten proteins in wheat, barley, rye and oats by genetically restricted major histocompatibility (MHC) receptors. This results in damage to villi which are present in the small intestine. CeD has many clinical manifestations, ranging from severe malabsorption to minimally symptomatic presentations. In India, the prevalence value is 8.5% (75-100th percentile). Currently, in India there's only one celiac database owned and maintained by Deenanath Mangeshkar Hospital (DMH), where they collect the information of the patient whose having CeD, but this database is not accessible to the public. Medical practitioners find it very difficult to identify what causes CeD. This One-Stop Database will help the researchers to go a step further to help them diagnose the patients and treat them accordingly based on the empirical data present. Our database provides information about peptides, proteins, genes, SNPs, -omics and clinical trials that might pose a risk of eliciting CeD. The ultimate goal is to create awareness, data regarding CeD available thus far, feasibility to research based on the data available and make it accessible not only to medical practitioners but to the public as well.
